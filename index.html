Smart Mobile Application for Enhancing Sri Lankan Betel Leaf Supply Chain

Modernizing the Betel Industry through Deep Learning and Market Intelligence.

Learn More
Overview
About the Project

This project introduces an integrated mobile solution designed to digitize and optimize the Sri Lankan betel leaf supply chain. By leveraging advanced CNNs, the application automates variety and commercial category identification, detects multi-class diseases, assesses propagation levels, and provides data-driven quality grading and price predictions.

The system empowers farmers, traders, and exporters across Sri Lanka with accessible expert knowledge, contributing to improved quality standards and sustainable growth within the betel farming industry.

Betel farming is a significant agricultural activity in Sri Lanka, yet farmers, traders, and exporters continue to face persistent challenges in variety identification, disease management, and quality-based pricing that are largely unaddressed by existing digital tools.

This application addresses five core areas: variety & commercial type identification, disease detection with propagation level assessment, tailored remedy suggestions, leaf quality identification, and price prediction.

A key innovation is that core models run fully offline using TensorFlow Lite — overcoming limited connectivity common in Sri Lankan agricultural field environments — with additional GPT-4o-mini AI advisory when online.

Literature Survey
🌿

No comprehensive digital solution exists for Sri Lankan betel farmers covering variety ID, disease management, and pricing together.

🤖

CNN models achieve up to 99% accuracy for crop disease detection (Mohanty et al.), yet none target Sri Lankan betel leaf diseases or severity staging.

📈

Bidirectional LSTM + SARIMA ensembles outperform traditional statistical models for volatile agricultural price prediction by capturing temporal and seasonal patterns.

🔬

Vision Transformers (ViTs) achieve 98.4% accuracy on vegetable quality grading — a technique adaptable for betel leaf quality classification with specialized preprocessing.

Research on betel leaf classification has explored both deep learning and traditional machine learning. Color and texture feature extraction combined with classical classifiers has proven effective for betel leaf type classification, improving explainability and aligning with traditional farmer knowledge.

For quality grading, early systems for fruits achieved over 90% accuracy using color, shape, and texture features with neural networks. Recent CNN and ViT models have further advanced this — providing the foundation for betel leaf grading with multi-class quality assessment and integrated price prediction.

Existing betel-specific disease studies (David & Mukunthan; Hasan et al.; Lakshmi & Nickolas) focused on only a few diseases, without real-field conditions, severity levels, or remedy recommendations. Maitipe et al. demonstrated 92% accuracy for severity staging but with a single model and static recommendations — limitations this system directly addresses.

Research Gap
The betel industry is held back by outdated methods, causing financial losses for farmers and inefficiencies in the supply chain — with no comprehensive technical solution currently available.

Classification & Grading Gap

Manual betel category classification relies on subjective judgment, causing inconsistent grading. No automated image-based system exists for Sri Lankan betel varieties and commercial categories (Peedunu, Kanda, Keti, Korikan). Lacks data integration and real-time platforms.

Quality & Pricing Gap

No studies address betel leaf quality and pricing together. Manual quality assessment leads to inconsistencies; regional variability is not modeled; and no data-driven price prediction models exist considering type, quality, region, and market type together.

Disease Severity & Remedy Gap

Existing systems identify diseases but cannot classify severity (Early, Moderate, Severe). Remedy approaches are static and rule-based, unable to adapt to disease stage. Very few solutions are developed specifically for Sri Lankan betel cultivation conditions.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to objectively classify betel leaf varieties, assess quality and predict price, identify diseases with severity levels, and deliver real-time expert guidance to Sri Lankan farmers?

  • Identification of premium varieties like 'Kalu Bulath' is done manually — causing subjective grading and pricing inconsistencies across the supply chain.
  • Diseases and pests are frequently misidentified, leading to ineffective treatment, crop losses, and financial harm for farmers.
  • Farmers have no access to real-time, objective data on quality or fair market prices across different regions and market types.
  • No existing system classifies disease severity or provides stage-specific, locally relevant remedy recommendations in Sinhala.

Proposed Solution

An offline-capable mobile app using CNNs identifies betel varieties (Maheneru, Galadalu) and commercial types (Kanda, Keti, Peedunu, Korikan), grades leaf quality (Premium / Standard / Poor), predicts market prices via a Bidirectional LSTM+SARIMA ensemble, detects 6 diseases via a hybrid VGG16+DenseNet121 model, and classifies disease severity with expert-guided Sinhala-language remedies enhanced by GPT-4o-mini when online.

Research Objectives

Variety & Commercial Category Identification

Develop a deep learning system to accurately identify Sri Lankan betel leaf types (Maheneru, Galadalu) and classify them into commercial categories — Peedunu, Kanda, Keti, and Korikan — using EfficientNetB0 with two-phase transfer learning.

Quality Assessment & Price Prediction

Accurately grade betel leaf quality (Premium, Standard, Poor — including Kalu Bulath identification) using EfficientNetV2-M, and predict fair market prices based on type, quality, region, and market using a Bidirectional LSTM+SARIMA ensemble trained on 9,429 market records.

Disease Identification

Develop a hybrid VGG16+DenseNet121 deep learning model that accurately identifies and classifies 6 betel leaf diseases — Brown Spot, Bacterial Blight, Leaf Spot, Caterpillar Damage, Kanamadiri Haniya, Red Spider Mite — plus healthy and dry leaves, with multi-label detection for co-occurring diseases.

Severity Classification & Remedy Recommendation

Develop a mobile AI module using ConvNeXtTiny (78.07% accuracy) to stage betel leaf disease propagation into Early, Moderate, and Severe levels, and provide expert-validated Sinhala-language remedy recommendations — offline via an 18-combination rule-based engine and enhanced online via GPT-4o-mini with gTTS audio output.

Methodology

The system consists of four integrated AI components deployed as an offline-capable mobile application using TensorFlow Lite for on-device inference:

  1. Variety & Commercial Category Identification — EfficientNetB0 two-phase transfer learning; 1,868 variety images + 1,618 commercial category images; class-imbalance addressed via stratified splits and inverse-frequency class weights.
  2. Quality Assessment & Price Prediction — EfficientNetV2-M for quality grading (2,274 images, 3 classes); Bidirectional LSTM+SARIMA ensemble for price prediction from 8,944 cleaned market records (Jan 2016–present).
  3. Disease Identification — Hybrid parallel VGG16+DenseNet121 dual-branch model with sigmoid output for multi-label disease detection across 6 disease classes; augmented with synthetic multi-label blending for co-occurring diseases.
  4. Severity Classification & Remedy — ConvNeXtTiny two-phase transfer learning (78.07% accuracy); 18-combination offline rule-based knowledge base; online GPT-4o-mini advisory with Sinhala gTTS audio output.
